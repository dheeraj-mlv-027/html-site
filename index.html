Overview

Executive Summary

The Alumni Pathfinder Dashboard is an innovative data analytics platform designed to bridge the gap between current students and alumni career trajectories. By aggregating and visualizing comprehensive alumni data including career paths, locations, companies, and departments, the platform provides actionable insights that help students make informed decisions about their academic and professional futures. Through interactive visualizations and robust search capabilities, students can explore real-world career outcomes, identify trends in their fields of interest, and connect with alumni who have walked similar paths. This project demonstrates the power of data-driven decision-making in higher education, offering a scalable solution that enhances student engagement, improves career services, and strengthens alumni networks.

Problem Context & Stakeholders

The Problem

Students lack visibility into real career outcomes of alumni, making it difficult to make informed decisions about academic paths, career choices, and networking opportunities. Traditional career services often provide generic advice without data-backed insights into actual alumni trajectories.

üë•

Who Is Affected?

  • Current Students: Need career guidance and mentorship connections
  • Career Services: Require data to provide better counseling
  • Alumni Relations: Want to strengthen engagement and networking
  • University Administration: Need insights for program improvement

Why It Matters

With rising education costs and competitive job markets, students need concrete data to maximize their ROI on education. Alumni networks are valuable but underutilized resources. This platform transforms raw alumni data into actionable career intelligence.

Technical Design

Architecture & Data Design

System Architecture

The Alumni Pathfinder platform follows a modern three-tier architecture with clear separation of concerns across business, information systems, and technology layers.

Enterprise Architecture

Business Architecture

Defines core business processes including student and alumni operations, user batch management, dashboard analytics, alumni search, engagement and networking, and analytics dashboard with insights.

Information Systems Architecture

Built on Streamlit framework with modular components for user interface, data processing, and API integrations. Core files include the main application, role-based CSV services, and clean data handling.

Technology Architecture

Leverages Python 3.11 runtime, Streamlit Cloud for deployment, local filesystems for data persistence, and comprehensive security measures including HTTPS, password hashing, and SSL encryption.

Entity-Relationship Diagram

Database Schema

The data model consists of five core entities:

  • Users: Authentication and role management (id, name, email, password_hash, role)
  • Profiles: User profile information (user_id, bio, profile_picture_url, linkedin_url, phone, location)
  • Student Profiles: Current student data (student_id_number, department, year_of_study, expected_graduation)
  • Alumni Profiles: Graduate information (alumni_id_number, department, graduation_year, degree_obtained)
  • Job History: Career trajectory data (alumni_profile_id, company_name, job_title, employment_type, location, start_date, end_date, description, skills)

Reflection

Lessons Learned & Team Reflection

Technical Learnings

  • Data Quality Matters: Spent significant time cleaning and validating alumni data. Learned the importance of establishing data quality standards early in the project.
  • Scalability Considerations: Initial design didn't account for 3000+ records. Had to optimize queries and implement pagination for better performance.
  • User-Centric Design: Early prototypes were too complex. User testing revealed the need for simpler navigation and clearer visualizations.
  • Framework Selection: Streamlit proved excellent for rapid prototyping but had limitations for complex interactions. Would consider React for production version.

Project Management

  • Agile Methodology: Two-week sprints with daily standups kept team aligned and enabled quick pivots when needed.
  • Clear Communication: Regular check-ins and documentation prevented misunderstandings and duplicate work.
  • Scope Management: Had to cut features like direct messaging and event calendar to meet deadlines. Learned to prioritize ruthlessly.
  • Risk Mitigation: Identified data privacy concerns early and allocated time for security implementation.

Stakeholder Engagement

  • Early Validation: Engaged career services staff early to understand real needs. Their input shaped core features.
  • Student Feedback: Conducted three rounds of user testing with students. Each round revealed new usability improvements.
  • Alumni Involvement: Alumni were enthusiastic about participating but concerned about privacy. Led to enhanced privacy controls.
  • Administration Buy-in: Regular demos to university leadership secured support and potential funding for expansion.

Challenges Overcome

  • Data Collection: Difficulty obtaining complete alumni data from disparate sources. Developed automated scrapers with privacy safeguards.
  • Performance Issues: Large dataset caused slow load times. Implemented caching and lazy loading to improve experience.
  • Privacy Concerns: Balancing transparency with privacy required multiple design iterations and legal consultation.
  • Team Coordination: Distributed team across time zones made synchronous work challenging. Async communication and clear documentation helped.

Team Reflection

This project pushed our team to think critically about the intersection of data, technology, and social impact. We entered with a straightforward goal‚Äîbuild an alumni dashboard‚Äîbut quickly realized the complexity of creating a truly useful tool.

Each team member brought unique strengths: some excelled at data engineering, others at visualization design, and some at stakeholder communication. We learned to leverage these differences rather than fight them. Our most productive moments came when we embraced diverse perspectives and challenged each other's assumptions.

The most valuable lesson was understanding that technology alone doesn't solve problems‚Äîit enables solutions when combined with deep user understanding, ethical considerations, and iterative refinement. We're proud not just of what we built, but of how we built it: collaboratively, thoughtfully, and with users at the center.
